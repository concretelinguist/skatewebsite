Wheel Diameter

Size (in mm) Functionality
50-53 mm Usually for tricks on street, park, and bowl; slower and performs poorly on rough ground
54-59 mm Average wheel size and used for street, park, bowl, and vert; faster
60+ mm Seen on longboards, old school boards, downhill boards; fastest and handles rough ground

Durometer

Durometer refers to the measured hardness of the wheel. Wheels come in the range of 78a-103a. When
choosing the wheel durometer, consider how you plan to skate the most - as wheel hardness affects the ride greatly.

Hardness Functionality Grip Noise
78a-87a Most suitable for rough roads, rolls the longest, softest Strong grip Quietest
88a-95a Suitable for street skating and cruising on less smooth ground Some grip Slightly Louder
96a-99a Most common in street skating on smooth ground, fairly hard, does not have as much rebound Less grip Quite Loud
101a + pro wheels; not good on slick or rough ground; extremely fast on smooth surfaces Much less grippy Very loud on rough ground
83b-84b Goes beyond the 'a' scale to describe the most hardest wheels Least grip of all Very loud on rough ground

Contact Patch

Generally, wheels with a wider contact patch exhibit more grip and stability while
wheels with smaller contact patches slide more easily. It is common for softer wheels
(cruiser and filmer wheels) to have a wider patch to give the most stable ride possible.
On the other hand, on most street and park wheels of higher durometers, contact patch is
affected by the shape of the wheel - though they often come with a narrow contact patch.
This allows for easier slides (less grip) and pivots.
